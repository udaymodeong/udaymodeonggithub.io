Japan

Japan is a country where ancient traditions meet cutting-edge innovation, offering tourists a unique and captivating experience. In bustling cities like Tokyo, visitors can explore futuristic districts filled with towering skyscrapers, high-tech gadgets, and neon lights, while just a short distance away, tranquil temples and serene gardens offer moments of reflection and peace. Kyoto, often regarded as the cultural heart of Japan, invites travelers to step back in time with its beautiful temples, traditional tea houses, and the iconic Arashiyama Bamboo Grove.

Mount Fuji, or Fujisan as the Japanese call it, is located on Japan’s largest island of Honshu. It has an elevation of 3,776m / 12,389ft and is Japan’s tallest peak. It is a dormant volcano with the last eruption taking place in 1707.​Mt.

Standing 333 meters high in central Tokyo, Tokyo Tower (東京タワー) is the world's tallest, self-supported steel tower and is 3 meters taller than its model, the Eiffel Tower. A symbol of Japan's post-war rebirth as a major economic power when it was surpassed by the Tokyo Skytree.
Kyoto is known for its numerous temples and shrines and is the type of city where you can come across beautiful temples and shrines while simply walking around. One of the most popular, Kiyomizu-dera, was one of my favorites.
